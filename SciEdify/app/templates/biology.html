Cell biology

Cell biology is a branch of biology that studies structure, function and behaviour of cells which are the basic units of life, responsible for the living and functioning organisms. There are two types of cells: Prokaryotic and Eukaryotic. They both reproduce through binary fission (division). There are three mandatory organelles that every cell of both types has – cell membrane, cytoplasm and genetic information. Prokaryotic cells are the smaller and simpler ones. They were the first cells to arise on Earth. Their genetic information (DNA) is placed directly in the cytoplasm and not in a cell nucleus. Another thing they do not have are mitochondria. This type of cells is found in bacteria and some single-celled organisms. Eukaryotic cells are much more complex. Their genetic information is positioned in a cell nucleus, and they have mitochondria. These cells compose animals, plants, fungi, some single-celled organisms and of course humans.

Animal cell

Animal cells are the cells that humans and animals consist of. They are Eukaryotic and here is their structure: ⦿ Cell membrane A lipid bilayer which has selective permeability which means not all substances will be able to pass through. In the membrane there is cholesterol that provides integrity and support. ⦿ Nucleus Regarded as the control center of a cell because it contains most of the genetic material. The nucleus regulates metabolism, growth and reproduction of the cell. ⦿ Cytoplasm The material, enclosed by the cell membrane, except for the nucleus. It contains water, the organelles, cytosol and cytoplasmic inclusions. ⦿ Endoplasmic reticulum (ER) The transportation system of the cell. It is made up of two subunits: rough ER, involved in protein synthesis and smooth ER, involved in lipid synthesis. ⦿ Golgi apparatus Packages and secrets biomolecules, sending them to their destination. ⦿ Mitochondrion The powerhouse of the cell. It generates ATP which is used by the cell as a source of chemical energy. ⦿ Lysosome The waste disposal system of the cell. It contains digestive enzymes and so its main function is degradation of polymers. ⦿ Ribosome Links amino acids together to form polypeptide chains a. k. a. proteins.

Plant cell

Plant cells are Eukaryotic as well, but they differ from animal ones. They are made of: ⦿ Cell wall The outer layer of a plant cell, responsible for the cell's strength, rigidity and resiliency against mechanical stress. ⦿ Cell membrane ⦿ Nucleus ⦿ Cytoplasm ⦿ Endoplasmic reticulum ⦿ Mitochondrion ⦿ Golgi apparatus ⦿ Ribosome ⦿ Lysosome ⦿ Vacuole The largest structure in the plant cell. It contains water, organic and inorganic molecules. A vacuole's functions vary but in general it isolates harmful materials and waste products, contains water and regulates pressure in the cell. ⦿ Chloroplast Organelle that contains chlorophyll and plays a very important role in photosynthesis. It converts salt, water and carbon dioxide into oxygen, glucose and other organic materials, using light energy.
